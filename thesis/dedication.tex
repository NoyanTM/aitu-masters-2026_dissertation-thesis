\chapter{Dedication and acknowledgements}
\begin{SingleSpace}
We applied multiple open source tools that have invaluable contributions and support for the development of this project. It is worth mentioning that there is a clear continuity between this research and my previous studies. Firstly, I was among the first in Kazakhstan to propose and evaluate the applicability of SOC (security operational center) automation through the integration of language model-driven bots within SIEM (security information and event management) systems for client reporting across various communication channels and incident response during my internship at MSSP GLOBAL in 2022-2023. Secondly, during my work at the Financial Monitoring Agency of Kazakhstan in 2023-2024, I addressed the potential for enhancing language model paradigms through retrieval-augmented generation, vector embedding of textual data, multimodal prototypes combining vision and language models, and knowledge graphs to achieve more stable response generation and accurate data retrieval. Thirdly, my bachelor's degree thesis in cybersecurity in 2024 examined the information security risks associated with AI models by proposing the use of local models instead of third-party services, data anonymization, and regulations as a component of the CIA triad, model training and fine-tuning, and general insights within the field of natural language processing.
\end{SingleSpace}
